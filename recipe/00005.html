Crispy Chicken Thighs with Peppers and Salsa Verde

Here's a recipe for sauce lovers - Let's bring texture and veggies to our chicken dish. Keeping it nice and moist! (See how many times you can say that without someone hitting you)

PREP TIME: 20 m
READY IN: 50 m
COOK TIME: 30 m
SERVES: 4

Ingredients

•

Low-Sodium Chicken Broth - 1 1/4 cups

•

Box Roasted Garlic-and-Olive Oil Cous Cous - 1 box

•

Vegetable Oil - 2 tsp

•

Skin-on, Bone-in Chicken Thighs - 6 large pieces

•

Kosher Salt - 1 1/2 tsp

•

Freshly Ground Pepper - 3/4 tsp

•

Colorful Bell Peppers - 3 medium sized

•

Sweet Onion - 1/2 medium sized

•

Colve Garlic - 2 pieces

•

Hot sauce - to taste

For Caper Salsa Verde

•

Fresh Parsley - 1/4 cup

•

Fresh Basil - 1/4 cup

•

Green Onion - 1 piece

•

Extra-virgin Olive Oil - 1/4 cup

•

Capers - 2 tbs

•

Fresh Lemon Juice - 1 1/2 tbs

•

Salt

•

Pepper

Instructions

Prepping the Chicken

1.

Preheat oven to 425 degrees F. Bring broth to a boil in a 12-inch cast iron skillet. Pour over couscous in a medium bowl; cover and set aside.

2.

Heat oil in skillet over medium-high heat until very hot. Sprinkle chicken with salt and pepper. Place chicken in skillet, skin sides down, and cook 10 minutes or until skin is browned and crispy. Turn chicken over and cook 4 minutes. Transfer chicken to a plate; discard drippings.

3.

Fluff couscous with a fork. Serve chicken and peppers on couscous, and top with desired amount of salsa.

4.

Stir together parsley, basil, green onion, olive oil, capers, and lemon juice. Season to taste with salt and pepper.

5.

Place chicken in the refrigerator so it marinates overnight.

Cooking

1.

Preheat the oven to 350 degrees.

2.

Place the chicken in a baking pan. I’d recommend placing the skin down for the thighs.

3.

Add a quarter stick of butter, I’d recommend cutting it into small pieces and strategically place then around the chicken.

4.

Add 1/2 cup of water to the pan and 2 tablespoons of apple cider vinegar for flavor.

5.

Cover the baking pan in aluminum foil.

6.

Bake the chicken on one side for an hour. After the initial hour remove the aluminum foil. Turn the chicken so the other side is down. Bake for another hour.

7.

After two hours of cooking they should be done!
