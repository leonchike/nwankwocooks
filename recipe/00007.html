Linguine Pasta with Shrimp, Tomatoes and Bratwurst

Want to dress up your pasta night? This is a delicious way to introduce depth and texture into an otherwise boring spaghetti night. You shouldn't have to pick between seafood and meat, so start living the high life!

PREP TIME: 15 m
READY IN: 55 m
COOK TIME: 40 m
SERVES: 4

Ingredients

•

Olive Oil - 2 tbs

•

Minced Garlic - 3 cloves

•

Diced Tomatoes - 4 cups

•

Dry White Wine - 1 cup

•

Butter - 2 tbs

•

Linguine Pasta - 1 package (16 ounce)

•

Peeled and Deveined Medium Shrimp - 2 lbs

•

Bratwurst Links - 2 pieces

•

Olive Oil - 2 tbs

•

Spices to Taste - Cajun Seasoning salt, Black Pepper, Cayenne Pepper Hot Sauce, Other Hot Sauce, Celery Salt, Sage

Instructions

1.

Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Stir in the garlic; cook 2 minutes.

2.

Add the tomatoes, and wine. Bring to a simmer and cook 30 minutes, stirring frequently.

3.

Once the tomatoes have simmered into a sauce, stir in the butter and season with cajun seasoning salt, pepper, sage, and hot sauce.

4.

Fill a large pot with lightly-salted water; bring to a rolling boil; stir in the linguine and return to a boil.

5.

Cook the pasta uncovered, stirring occasionally, until the pasta has cooked through but is still firm to the bite, about 11 minutes. Drain well in a colander set in the sink.

6.

Season the shrimp with the Cajun seasoning and pepper. Heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.

7.

Cook the bratwurst after the shrimp is done - Season it only with celery salt and black pepper. Stir in the shrimp and cook until pink on the outside and no longer translucent in the center, about 5 minutes.

8.

Stir the shrimp into the pasta sauce, then stir the sauce into the linguine to serve. Season to taste if you want to add anything else.
