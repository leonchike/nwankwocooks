Crunchwrap Supreme

A home-cooked version of the fast food classic, this dish is very filling and gives you that taco bell fix without showing your face at the drive through!

PREP TIME: 20 m
READY IN: 26 m
COOK TIME: 6 m
SERVES: 4

Ingredients

•

Ground Beef - 1 lbs

•

Taco Meat Seasoning

•

Large Tortillas - 4

•

Small Tortillas - 4

•

Nacho Cheese Sauce

•

Tostada Shells - 4

•

Sour Cream - 1 cup

•

Shredded Lettuce - 2 cups

•

Tomatoes - 1 cup chopped

•

Mexican 4 Blend Cheese - 2 cups

•

Vegetable Oil - 1 tbs

•

Olive Oil - 2 tsp

•

Hot Sauce - To Taste

•

Ortega Taco Sauce Spicy - On the side

Instructions

1.

In a skillet over medium heat, heat a dash of olive oil. Add ground beef and brown until the beef is no longer pink, about 5 minutes. Drain liquid, then season with taco seasoning.

2.

Build crunchwraps: Lay out a large soft tortilla and add a large scoop of ground beef to the center. Make sure it is sized to the flat tostada hard shells; leaving a generous amount of space around the edges for folding. Drizzle the nacho cheese over the meat (more than you think you'll need), then place tostada shell on top. Spread sour cream over each shell, then top with lettuce, tomato, cheeses and your choice of hot sauce.

3.

Place the smaller flour tortilla in the center of each crunchwrap then tightly fold the edges of the large tortilla towards the center, creating pleats. After wrapping, flip crunchwraps when placing into the pan so the pleats are on the bottom and they stay together.

4.

Cook crunchwraps: In a medium-sized nonstick pan over medium heat, heat a thin layer of vegetable oil. Place crunchwrap seam-side down and cook until tortilla is golden on the bottom, about 3 minutes. Flip crunchwrap and cook until the other side is golden, about 2:30 minutes more.

5.

Serve with the Ortega taco sauce on the side
